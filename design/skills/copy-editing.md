# Copy editing — the seven sweeps

Sequential single-dimension passes over existing copy. After each sweep,
re-check the earlier ones; stop when a full pass finds nothing new. Never
change the core message while editing.

1. **Clarity** — jargon, unclear pronouns, sentences doing too much,
   buried points. One idea per section; copy speaks TO the reader.
2. **Voice** — read it aloud; catch register shifts and "we/the company"
   mixing.
3. **So what** — every claim must survive "okay, so what?" or gain a
   "which means…" bridge to a reader benefit.
4. **Prove it** — "trusted by thousands (which thousands?)",
   "industry-leading (says who?)": attach real proof or soften the claim.
5. **Specificity** — replace vague quantities with numbers and timeframes;
   anything that cannot be made specific is filler — delete it.
6. **Emotion** — paint the before-state concretely; emotion serves the
   message, never decorates it.
7. **Zero risk** — list every reason to hesitate near each CTA; answer
   with guarantee / free tier / "no credit card" / clear next step.

## Word-level pass

- Cut: very, really, just, actually, basically, "in order to", things,
  stuff. Delete-entirely: "at the end of the day", "last but not least".
- Swap: utilize→use, leverage→use, facilitate→help, implement→set up,
  innovative→new, robust→strong, seamless→smooth (see also the banned
  buzzword list in `copy-craft.md` — those never appear at all).
- Active voice; front-load the key info; sentences ≤25 words; paragraphs
  2-4 sentences.

## Panel gate (high-stakes pages only)

Score the draft as 3-5 personas — target customer, conversion strategist,
brand editor, compliance/claims reviewer — each 1-10 with critiques.
Revise lowest-first until every persona ≥7 and the average ≥8. Mandatory
for launch copy, pricing pages, and high-traffic landings. (This is the
copy analogue of the design critique jury — Phase B wires it into the
engine.)
