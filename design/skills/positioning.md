# Positioning & product-marketing context

The upstream skill every copy task reads first. A page written without
positioning is a template with nouns swapped in.

## The context document

Keep one versioned positioning doc per brand (ours lives in
`.claude/skills/brandkite-copy/SKILL.md`; customers' come from their
`brandContext` module). It answers:

- **Category** — what shelf customers search when they look for this.
- **Audience & job-to-be-done** — who, and the #1 frustration that brings
  them here (their words, not ours).
- **Competitive frame** — direct / secondary (different solution, same
  problem) / indirect, and how each falls short.
- **Differentiators** — what the alternatives lack; why switchers stay.
- **Objections & anti-persona** — reasons a qualified buyer hesitates;
  who this is NOT for.
- **Four forces** — push away from the current solution, pull toward
  this one, habit keeping them stuck, anxiety about switching.
- **Verbatim customer language** — exact phrases to use and avoid.
- **Proof points & claims policy** — every material claim maps to product
  behavior, data, or an attributed quote; everything else gets softened
  or cut. "Best" needs a ranking; "fastest" needs a benchmark;
  "trusted by thousands" needs the number.

## Method rules

- Push for exact customer phrases over polished descriptions.
- Ask "what's the #1 frustration that brings them to you?" — not
  "what problem do you solve?"
- Repositioning is a versioned event: downstream copy generates against
  this document, so substantive changes must be flagged.
