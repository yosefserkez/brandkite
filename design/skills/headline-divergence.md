# Headline divergence

Runtime fragment: `HEADLINE_DIVERGENCE` in `convex/lib/design/skills.ts`.

Variants must take materially different ANGLES — a different opening
thought — not minor rewrites of one idea. Six angles:

1. **Outcome-led** — "{Outcome} without {pain}", "Turn {input} into
   {outcome}", "{Outcome} in {timeframe}".
2. **Pain-led** — "Never {unpleasant event} again", "Stop {pain}. Start
   {benefit}.", or the pain as a question.
3. **Mechanism-led** — "{Outcome} by {mechanism}"; the how is the hook.
4. **Identity-led** — "The {category} for {audience}"; "You don't have to
   be {expert} to {outcome}".
5. **Proof-led** — "{Number} {people} use {product} to {outcome}" — ONLY
   with a real number from the supplied context.
6. **Contrarian** — "The {opposite-of-usual} way to {outcome}"; "The
   {category} that {differentiator}".

Rules:

- ≤10 words (6-8 ideal); specific beats clever; contains the audience or
  their goal.
- "50% faster" beats "blazing fast" — but only if the number is real.
- A set of variants that could be reordered without anyone noticing has
  failed the divergence test.

CTA copy: action verb + what they get, first person. Weak: Submit,
Sign Up, Learn More, Get Started, Click Here. Strong: "Start my free
trial", "Get my brand kit", "See it on your site".
