# Brandkite v2 — committed direction (2026-07-18)

_Authoritative. Supersedes the v1 "agency wedge" framing in STRATEGY.md. Read this first for product direction._

## What we are
**The brand & identity layer for the AI-native company.** Where a company's brand is born, lives, and stays consistent as it grows — from idea to scale.

## The one-liner
"Your idea, launch-ready. A brand and a site you'd actually ship — in minutes. Kept consistent, forever."

## Target customer
The technical/scrappy **solo or very-early founder** (pre- or just-launched), not a designer, no agency budget, needs to look legitimate and get online fast. Same population Stripe Atlas / cofounder.co serve. (Secondary: tiny agencies/freelancers who make brands for such clients.)

## The job to be done
"Make my thing look like a real company so I'm not embarrassed to put it in front of customers/investors — fast, cheap, without being a designer." The emotional core: **legitimacy on day one.**

## The wedge (solve this ONE thing exceptionally well)
Turn an idea into **(a) a genuinely great, cohesive visual identity** (logo + color + type a non-designer would proudly ship) **and (b) a live, on-brand landing page** they can publish. "Look legit, get online, instantly."
- The **identity is the quality bar** — make-or-break for "not a gimmick." If the logo is meh, nothing else matters.
- The **live site is the flagship living output** — a real hosted page, not a text block. This is what makes Brandkite a *used service* (recurring, retained) instead of a one-shot download, and what earns a subscription.

## The moat
Best-in-class identity **quality** + a single **consistent source of truth** everything derives from. Deep where horizontal players (cofounder.co-style company-OSes) are shallow. They do "brand" as a thin slice of "selling"; we own brand and go a mile deep.

## Why not one-off / why it's infrastructure (the Stripe-Atlas arc)
Brand kit = the wedge (low commitment, impressive, gets them in). Lock-in = being the source of truth for identity and the place their public surfaces live and stay on-brand as they grow:
1. Day 1: brand + live landing page.
2. Launching: on-brand social, ads, launch announcement, email.
3. Hiring: brand guidelines + asset hub for teammates/contractors.
4. Scaling: **brand-as-API / design tokens** their product and tools pull from.
- Other tools (company-OSes, site/deck builders) integrate Brandkite as the brand primitive → we're the Stripe-of-brand *underneath* the stack, not just a direct app. Design toward this.

## Pricing (no-brainer, gated on the living output)
Compare to the alternative stack: Looka logo (~$20–65) + Squarespace (~$16/mo) + hours of copy, all disconnected. Brandkite gives the cohesive whole, hosted, in minutes.
- **Free:** generate + preview the full brand + a landing page (feel the "whoa"). Cannot publish/export.
- **Pro (~$19–29/mo):** publish the hosted site (custom domain), unlimited regenerations, export everything, edit anytime. Subscription justified by the **live site + ongoing edits** (fixes "why pay monthly for a one-shot").
- **Scale/API (later):** brand-as-API access, higher limits, team/asset hub.
- Quality IS the pricing strategy: a no-brainer only if the output clears the bar.

## What this changes about the product NOW
1. **Obsess over identity quality** — logo especially (variations, refinement, tasteful defaults, better model/prompting). Highest priority.
2. **Make the website a real, publishable, hosted page** — the flagship, not a studio card. Biggest gap between gimmick and product.
3. **Demote the strategy/text modules** (mission/vision/values/personas/story/tone) to optional depth — they're the slop risk and least valued at day 1. Front door = identity + site + go-to-market copy.
4. **Design a clean brand "source of truth"** and plan to expose it via API (scale/infra layer).
5. **Message it** founder-to-founder, outcome-first, zero jargon. Not "AI-powered brand identity platform."

## Hard questions to validate (don't skip)
- Will an early founder PAY, or only tourist the free tier? (WTP unproven — only "sale" was the owner's own test.)
- Is our logo/identity quality actually "I'd use this," not "cool demo"? (Test with real founders, brutal feedback.)
- Does "brand + live site" beat v0/Lovable/Wix-AI/Squarespace-AI for this person? Edge = brand-first + consistency — is it felt?
- Market = founder (volume, low WTP) or agency (fewer, repeat, higher WTP)?
